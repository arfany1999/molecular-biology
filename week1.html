1. DNA Structure

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms. It consists of two long strands that form a double helix. Each strand is made of nucleotides, which include a sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.

Components of DNA:

ComponentType
NucleotideBasic building block
Double HelixStructure formed by two strands

2. DNA Replication

DNA replication is the process by which DNA makes a copy of itself during cell division. Each strand of the original double helix serves as a template for the new strand.

Quiz Questions:

  1. What is the role of DNA polymerase?
  2. Explain the significance of the leading and lagging strands.

3. Transcription

Transcription is the process of copying a segment of DNA into RNA. It is the first step in the process of going from a gene to the corresponding protein.

Quiz Questions:

  1. What molecule is synthesized during transcription?
  2. What enzyme is responsible for transcription?

4. Translation

Translation is the process by which the sequence of bases in mRNA is translated into a sequence of amino acids to form a protein.

Quiz Questions:

  1. What is the role of ribosomes in translation?
  2. How does tRNA contribute to translation?
