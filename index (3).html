ONPS2431 — Mid-Semester Study Guide · Weeks 1–7

📖 About This Guide

This is your comprehensive, interactive study resource for the ONPS2431 mid-semester examination. It covers all content from Weeks 1–7 using multiple learning techniques: detailed explanations, diagrams, comparison tables, interactive quizzes, flashcards, step-by-step processes, and analogy-based learning. Work through each week systematically or jump to any topic using the sidebar.

Course Map

Week Topic Key Concepts Status
1 DNA & Central Dogma DNA structure, replication, transcription, translation —
2 Molecular Techniques Gel electrophoresis, restriction enzymes, vectors, PCR, blotting, miniprep —
3 Molecular Cloning Cloning workflow, selection & screening, blue-white screening —
4 Bacterial Gene Transfer Transformation, conjugation, transduction, lytic vs lysogenic —
5 Transgenic & GMOs Transgenic organisms, Bt crops, recombinant proteins —
6 Gene Therapy Somatic vs germline, viral/non-viral delivery, SCID case —
7 PCR Applications & qPCR qPCR, SYBR Green vs TaqMan, Ct values, RT-PCR —

How to Use This Guide

  1. Read each topic page — core content is presented with text, diagrams, and tables. Key terms are highlighted in purple.
  2. Study the diagrams — interactive and static diagrams help you visualize processes like replication, transcription, and cloning.
  3. Test yourself — every week ends with a Knowledge Check quiz. Aim for 100% before moving on.
  4. Use active recall — try to explain each concept from memory before re-reading. Use the flashcards to test yourself.
  5. Review the Glossary & Cheat Sheet — consolidate all key terms and processes before the exam.
⚠️ Study Tip

Don't just read passively. After each subtopic, close the page and write out what you remember on paper or a blank doc. Come back and fill gaps in a different colour. This is the single most effective study technique for exam performance.

Week 1: DNA & the Central Dogma

DNA structure, replication, transcription, and translation

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe the structure of DNA including nucleotide components, base pairing, and the double helix
  • Explain the process of semi-conservative DNA replication and the roles of key enzymes
  • Outline the steps of transcription from initiation to termination
  • Describe translation including the roles of mRNA, tRNA, and ribosomes
  • Summarise the Central Dogma of molecular biology and its significance

This week forms the foundation of everything else in this course. Every technique in Weeks 2–7 manipulates, copies, or analyses DNA. If you nail this week, the rest clicks into place.

Topics in This Week

SubtopicWhat You'll LearnTeaching Methods
DNA Structure Nucleotides, base pairing (A-T, G-C), antiparallel strands, major/minor grooves Diagrams, analogy, table
DNA Replication Semi-conservative model, origin of replication, leading/lagging strand, Okazaki fragments Step-by-step process, enzyme table, interactive diagram
Transcription RNA polymerase, promoter, template vs coding strand, mRNA processing Comparison table, process diagram
Translation Ribosomes, codons, tRNA, initiation/elongation/termination Interactive codon table, step process, analogy
Central Dogma Summary DNA → RNA → Protein, exceptions (reverse transcriptase) Flow diagram, summary table
